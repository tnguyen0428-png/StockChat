Stage 1 — v_signal_cohort_stats

The truth table. Nothing user-visible yet. This is what the algorithm reads before it decorates any alert.
View definition
create or replace view v_signal_cohort_stats as
-- One row per (signal_type, horizon). Recomputed live from finalized snapshots.
-- Only includes Polygon-scored rows (tracked_at >= 2026-04-14) so FMP-era
-- prices don't poison the cohort stats.
select
  a.signal_type,
  s.interval_key                                       as horizon,
  count(*)                                             as n_samples,
  round(avg((s.outcome = 'hit')::int) * 100, 1)     as hit_rate_pct,
  round(avg(s.return_pct)::numeric, 2)               as avg_return_pct,
  round(avg(s.return_pct) filter (where s.outcome = 'hit')::numeric,  2) as avg_win_pct,
  round(avg(s.return_pct) filter (where s.outcome = 'miss')::numeric, 2) as avg_loss_pct,
  max(s.tracked_at)                                    as last_refreshed
from alert_performance_snapshots s
join breakout_alerts a on a.id = s.alert_id
where s.outcome is not null
  and s.tracked_at >= '2026-04-14T00:00:00Z'
group by a.signal_type, s.interval_key;

n ≥ 50 & hit_rate < 55%

What this tells us before we build anything

SHIP gap_up and vol_surge at 1d and 7d. Sample sizes are well past the 50-row floor and the 1d hit rates (64% / 71%) are meaningful. These can drive decorated cards in Stage 3 on day one.

HOLD flow_signal cohort is borderline. 47 samples at 1d is below our 50-row cutoff. Either wait for the next 30 days of data or drop the sample-size floor to 40 and accept a wider confidence interval — but don't show a "68%" number on a card backed by 47 trades.

HOLD breakout_52w doesn't have enough history to score yet. Show it as an undecorated alert until cohort fills in. This is why "Insufficient" has to be a first-class state in the UI — not an edge case.

CONFIRMS 30d horizon is the weakest across every signal. Avg returns hover near zero, hit rates collapse toward 50/50. That validates leading with 1d + 7d on the card and leaving 30d for the detail sheet.

WATCH Asymmetry matters. vol_surge 1d wins +2.88% but loses −2.86% — roughly symmetric. gap_up 30d wins +7.92% and loses −7.88% — also symmetric but with much wider swings. This is the data that justifies showing "when wrong" on the card in Stage 4.
